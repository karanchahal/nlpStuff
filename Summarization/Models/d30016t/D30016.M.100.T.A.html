[1] Swiss Air flight 111 crashed off Nova Scotia on Sept. 2 , 1998 [2] killing all 129 passengers and crew . [3] The cause has not been determined , [4] but there was extreme heat damage to the front of the aircraft and it is suspected [5] that an in-flight entertainment system had electrical problems . [6] Swiss Air , while insisting that the system was installed correctly , [7] has disconnected the system in other aircraft as a precaution . [8] The airline immediately offered $ 20,000 to families of the victims [9] and , [10] having opted out of a $ 75,000 liability cap , [11] began work on a plan to provide compensation of approximately $ 137,000 per family .
