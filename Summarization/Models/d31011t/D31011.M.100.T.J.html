[1] Cooking oil prices rose sharply in the Philippines on speculation [2] that copra production will decline due to the effects of el Nino and recent typhoons on coconut crops . [3] Typhoon Babs , which caused 151 fatalities [4] and forced 400,000 people from their homes , [5] was particularly destructive in the Bicol region , a large coconut-production area . [6] After Babs , President Estrada declared four provinces and a city [7] to be calamity zones , eligible for emergency funds . [8] He also jump-started a fund for victims of the typhoons with a contribution equivalent to his yearly salary . [9] Babs subsequently swept through Taiwan , triggering landslides , and Hong Kong .
