[1] Philippine Airlines ( PAL ) experienced a crisis in 1998 . [2] Unable to make payments on a $ 2.1 billion debt , [3] it was faced by a pilot 's strike in June and the region 's currency problems [4] which reduced passenger numbers [5] and inflated costs . [6] On September 23 PAL shut down [7] after the ground crew union turned down a settlement [8] which it accepted two weeks later . [9] PAL resumed domestic flights on October 7 and international flights on October 26 . [10] Resolution of the basic financial problems was elusive , however , [11] and as of December 18 PAL was still $ 2.2 billion in debt [12] and losing close to $ 1 million a day .
