[1] Philippines Airlines ( PAL ) , Asia 's oldest airline , [2] devastated in 1998 by pilot and ground worker strikes and with a rising $ 2.1 billion debt , stopped all operations for 13 days . [3] A 10-year , no-strike agreement was finally ratified [4] when the government began using foreign carriers for domestic flights . [5] PAL resumed domestic flights on October 7 and international flights slowly over the next weeks . [6] This shutdown had been another blow to the debt ridden national flag carrier . [7] Following the strike , efforts to revive the airlines met more obstacles . [8] Cathay Pacific Airways would only help [9] if the payroll , including 200 pilots hired during the pilot strike , were slashed .
