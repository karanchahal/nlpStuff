[1] The fate of Asia 's oldest airline , PAL , is uncertain . [2] Negotiations with Cathay Pacific Airways [3] to infuse $ 100 million dollars into the company collapsed [4] when PAL Chairman Tan refused to agree to major job cuts [5] and to relinquish management control to Cathay . [6] PAL is buried under a $ 2.2 billion dollar debt [7] it cannot repay and $ 1 million a day losses . [8] A replacement investor is unlikely [9] and President Estrada , though supported by Tan in the election , [10] has rejected a government bailout . [11] PAL 's financial troubles were exacerbated by a two-week shutdown in September due to a dispute with its largest union .
