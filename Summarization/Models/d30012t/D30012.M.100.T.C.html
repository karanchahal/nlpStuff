[1] The Leonid meteor showers occur every 33 years as the Tempel-Tuttle comet speeds through the inner solar system [2] shedding swarms of particles [3] as it nears the sun . [4] The spectacular 1966 display peaked at 150,000 meteors per hour . [5] As the 1998 showers approached , scientists disagreed about the vulnerability of the 600 odd commercial and military earth orbit satellites . [6] Precautions were taken to place satellites in a protective modes or angles . [7] The storm was disappointingly light compared to 1966 . [8] Afterwards , no problems were reported . [9] The viewing was best in Asia , poor in the States . [10] Scientists built unique equipment to conduct their experiments .
