[1] The Earth encounters Leonid meteors every year on about the same date , [2] but only once in approximately every 33 years do heavy bombardments occur . [3] The meteors are mostly microscopic fragments of Comet 55P/Tempel-Tuttle . [4] This year , Asians were treated to the most spectacular view . [5] Meteor sightings peaked at slightly more than 2,000 per hour [6] - barely one-tenth the rate at which meteors hit the atmosphere during the great 1966 Leonid storm . [7] Many of the world 's 600-plus satellites were maneuvered to reduce the possibility of damage from the speeding grit . [8] No problems were reported . [9] Scientists conducted many experiments to further knowledge about meteor storms .
