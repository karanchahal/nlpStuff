[1] Optimum viewing of the Leonid meteor shower in November 1998 was forecast for East Asia . [2] Some predicted the shower to rival the greatest of the century in 1966 , [3] others said it would be more modest . [4] Meteor showers are hard to predict . [5] Of particular concern in 1998 was the danger posed by the storms to earth-orbiting satellites . [6] Some satellites were repositioned to be less likely targets , [7] but authorities differed as to the real danger . [8] Scientists studied the showers with various airborne instruments . [9] In fact , the showers were more impressive viewed from Europe than East Asia and there was no damage to satellites .
