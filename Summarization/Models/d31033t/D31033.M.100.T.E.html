[1] Invoking the Sherman Act , [2] the Justice Department and 20 states have begun anti-trust proceedings against Microsoft Corporation . [3] Key to the government 's case is the allegation , supported by Netscape , [4] that Microsoft suggested in a June 1995 meeting [5] that Netscape join Microsoft in dividing the internet software market . [6] In a taped deposition , Microsoft Chairman Bill Gates denied any knowledge of such an offer . [7] Microsoft lawyers later produced an e-mail from Netscape President James Barkdale which suggests [8] Netscape was a willing participant in the internet software discussion . [9] Despite the on-going suit , Microsoft made an unsuccessful bid [10] to acquire the software business behind the Palm Pilot .
