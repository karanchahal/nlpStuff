[1] The government 's antitrust case against Bill Gates ' Microsoft Corporation cited aggressive tactics in attempting to acquire Palm Pilot . [2] The governments ' first salvo was to attack Gates ' contention [3] that he was not involved by showing documents [4] portraying his company obsessed with crushing its competitor . [5] Leading prosecution witness , Netscape CEO James Barksdale , cited blatant threats by Microsoft , [6] which Microsoft characterized as acceptable business practices . [7] They also claimed [8] that Netscape initiated some of the contacts . [9] Despite being the target of the government 's case , Microsoft earnings continued to grow . [10] In its defense , Microsoft said [11] that the `` New Economy '' was also on trial [12] and that new antitrust definitions were needed .
