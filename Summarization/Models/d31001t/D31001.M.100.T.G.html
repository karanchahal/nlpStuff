[1] A South African panel probing the apartheid-era abuses is releasing its final report [2] following a 2-year investigation . The panel , the Truth and Reconciliation Commission [3] ( ARC ) , found [4] that most violations of human rights were through government security and law enforcement agencies . [5] However , the panel has also accused the African National Congress ( ANC ) of human rights violations . [6] The last apartheid president , F. W. de Klerk , will not be implicated in the rights violations after threatening a court challenge . [7] The goal of the ARC is to create an idealistic reconciliation of all citizens through amnesty [8] rather than hold politically divisive trials .
