[1] The South African Truth and Reconciliation Commission , [2] chaired by Archbishop Desmond Tutu , [3] completed a lengthy study of human rights violations , [4] including torture and murder , in that country , 1960-1994 . [5] The apartheid government was the most culpable [6] but the African National Congress also had a share of the blame . [7] The `` truth '' part of the report was the easiest : [8] it listed crimes and named names , [9] including former president Botha , Inkatha leader Buthelezi , and even Winnie Mandela . [10] Information implicating former president de Klerk was withheld . [11] Reconciliation promised to be more difficult . [12] Amnesty was proposed for those [13] who came forward ( perhaps within a given period ) , [14] confessed , and requested amnesty .
