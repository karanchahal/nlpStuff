[1] The Truth and Reconciliation Commission , [2] headed by Bishop Desmond Tutu , [3] has issued its report on human rights violations in South Africa during apartheid . [4] The report , which covers the years from 1960 to 1994 , [5] lays a major portion of the blame on the ruling South African government during the period . [6] All political parties , especially the African National Congress , also were accused of involvement in human rights violations . [7] Former President de Klerk , [8] who began the process to end apartheid in South Africa , [9] was successful in having his name suppressed in the report . [10] The African National Council also was considering bringing suit [11] to have details of its role suppressed .
