[1] South Africa 's Truth and Reconciliation Commission was established [2] to clear the air after the ugly apartheid struggle . [3] Retired Archbishop Tutu headed the Commission . [4] It worked two plus years , [5] holding countrywide meetings . [6] It was controversial from the start . [7] Commission leaks prompted de Klerk , the last white President , to threaten a court challenge [8] The final 3,500-page report was issued on 29 October 1998 . [9] The white majority received the greater blame for rights violations , [10] but the black ANC was also cited . [11] De Klerk 's name did not appear in the report . [12] Botha , the Inkatha Party , the ruling ANC , and Winnie Mandela were specifically cited .
