[1] Brazilian President Fernando Henrique Cardoso was re-elected in the first round of the election with 56 percent of the vote , [2] eliminating the need for a runoff . [3] Cardoso easily defeated three-time presidential candidate Luiz Inacio Lula de Silva , [4] who garnered only about 34 percent of the vote . [5] The election , [6] held as Brazil was in the midst of an economic crisis , [7] ensured that continuity of leadership would be maintained . [8] Newly re-elected President Cardoso delayed announcing plans for economic reform until after the gubernatorial elections . [9] Those elections , [10] which saw many rivals elected , [11] cast doubt on the future of Cardoso 's economic reforms .
