[1] Pope John Paul II paid a three-day visit to Croatia in November 1998 . [2] The primary purpose of this , his second , visit was the beatification ( the last step before sainthood ) of Cardinal Stepinac , [3] who died in 1960 . [4] The Pope declared Stepinac a martyr to the atrocities of Communism , [5] but many non-Catholics considered him a nazi sympathizer during World War II. [6] Almost 500,000 people attended the ceremony in Marija Bistrica . [7] The Pope later met with President Tudjman in Zagreb [8] and said a final mass in Split , [9] where he called for reconciliation , a renewal of values , [10] and increased international aid to the war-torn Balkans .
