[1] Beginning November 16 , a frigid Arctic air mass brought unusually cold weather [2] lasting several weeks to most of Europe . [3] Below normal temperatures were experienced in France , Romania , Bulgaria , Poland and Russia . [4] Rare snowfall occurred in southern Italy . [5] Throughout Poland , Europe 's most affected country , the death toll reached 85 . [6] Most deaths were blamed on homelessness and drinking alcohol . [7] In Russian , at least 39 people died in Moscow alone ; [8] an addition 143 people were hospitalized with hypothermia and 51 with frostbite . [9] Throughout Europe transportation was disrupted [10] and weather related accidents occurred . [11] The prolonged sub-zero temperatures made this Europe 's coldest November in many , many years .
