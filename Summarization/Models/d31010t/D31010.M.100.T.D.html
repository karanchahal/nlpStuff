[1] A killer cold wave hit much of Europe in November 1998 , [2] lasting more than four weeks in some areas . [3] Romania and Poland suffered most . [4] France , Bulgaria , Italy , Croatia and Russia also experienced bitter cold . [5] Temperatures reached as low as minus 26 degrees Celsius . [6] In Romania cold and blizzard conditions closed major roadways , [7] and dozens of trains were canceled . [8] Communities were without electricity . [9] The death toll was staggering . [10] At least 39 died in Moscow . [11] The death toll in Poland reached 85 in the four weeks after 16 November . [12] Some died in storm-related accidents . [13] Most victims were homeless , or those suffering from alcoholism .
