[1] For four days in September 1998 Hurricane Georges plowed through the Caribbean [2] affecting more than 30 million people . [3] A high pressure system kept the storm on its east-to-west course . [4] More than 500 were killed , [5] more than 138,000 left homeless and property damage exceeded $ 5 billion . [6] Authorities in Haiti and the Dominican Republic seemed unprepared as reflected in death rates of 221 and more than 280 respectively . [7] Widespread destruction of homes in Haiti and Puerto Rico presented major problems of funding because of the number of uninsured homes and the lack of local financial resources , [8] but U.S. and international assistance was quickly arranged .
