[1] At fiscal year 's end on Sept. 30 , 1998 , Congress had not approved 13 necessary spending bills , [2] so voted emergency funding to keep the government running through Oct. 9th , then Oct. 12th . [3] Members were eager to campaign at home for re-election [4] but also to ensure funding for pet projects . [5] Some bills risked veto because of anti-environmental riders . [6] Several bills combined into an omnibus bill would make veto difficult . [7] Republicans , vowing to avoid another shutdown that would distract attention from impeachment , [8] became willing to resolve spending issues [9] and dropped huge tax cut demands . [10] Final unresolved issues included funding for the IMF and emergencies , and census technique . [11] Clinton consulted other governments on proposed IMF changes .
