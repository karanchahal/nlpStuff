[1] The Turkish government of Prime Minister Mesut Yilmaz fell after mob-tie allegations . [2] Turkey 's constitution mandates a secular republic despite its Muslim majority . [3] Military and secular leaders pressured President Demirel to keep the Islamic-oriented Virtue Party on the fringe . [4] Business leaders feared Virtue would alienate the EU . [5] Virtue 's leader ceded its right as Parliament 's largest party to lead the government . [6] Bulent Ecevit , leader of the Democratic Left Party and former PM , asked Yilmaz and True Path Party head Tansu Ciller to join him in a secular coalition , creating a large Parliamentary majority . [7] A political vacuum risked Islamic ascension [8] and endangered a Kurdish rebel 's extradition . [9] Demirel was expected to name Ecevit PM .
