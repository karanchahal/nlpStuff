[1] In late 1998 the centrist government of Turkey led by Mesut Yilmaz collapsed in a corruption scandal . [2] President Demirel approached Recai Kutan , leader of a pro-Islamic party with the most seats in parliament , to lead a new government . [3] Kutan demurred from even being part of the new government . [4] Next Demirel talked with Yilmaz rival , Tansu Ciller , leader of a center-right party tainted by the same corruption charges . [5] Ciller endorsed a broad-based coalition . [6] Bulent Ecevit , leader of a left-center party , then declared for a coalition with Yilmaz and Ciller . [7] Demirel scheduled a meeting with Ecevit , [8] suggesting that Ecevit might be the next Prime Minister .
