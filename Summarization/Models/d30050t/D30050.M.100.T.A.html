[1] As the 1998 mid-term elections approached , [2] it seemed that Republicans might gain a filibuster-proof 60 seat majority in the senate . [3] Four Democratic incumbents seemed to be in real danger [4] while two Republicans seemed to have the edge in contests to fill vacancies previously held by Democrats . [5] A strong bid by the Democratic challenger to the Republican incumbent in New York did not seem enough to avoid strong Republican gains . [6] The Monica Lewinsky scandal and the impeachment inquiry in the house also seemed to bode ill for the Democrats and Democratic house candidates struggled with the question whether to support or oppose impeachment .
