[1] The United Nations Security Council established a War Crimes Tribunal in 1993 to investigate and prosecute crimes [2] arising from the breakup of Yugoslavia . [3] In October 1998 , the tribunal secured its third conviction [4] when a Bosnian Serb plead guilty to 13 murders . [5] Also that month , the trial of four for 1992 atrocities in a Bosnian prison camp was nearing completion . [6] Also , Kosovo became an area of contention . [7] The Yugoslavian government was claiming the tribunal had no jurisdiction over the 9-month rebellion [8] and was refusing investigators visas . [9] The U.N. tribunal sought to enter the province [10] and investigate atrocities reported by both sides .
