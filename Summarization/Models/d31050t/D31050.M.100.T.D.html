[1] In October 1988 China signed the International Covenant on Civil and Political Rights . [2] Perhaps emboldened by that , dissidents pushed for the recognition of a new China Democratic Party . [3] The government , citing a long-standing policy , [4] said that it would not tolerate another political party . [5] Three prominent dissidents , one of them Wu Xenli , [6] working to establish the new party were arrested . [7] They were accused of endangering national security , [8] and faced prison terms of five years . [9] Prospective defense lawyers were intimidated . [10] The United States criticized the arrests , [11] and an embassy official was rebuffed [12] when he tried to observe a trial .
